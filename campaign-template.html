Why This Problem Caught My Eye

How you found this company. What you noticed that made you stop and look closer. The specific signal — a confusing homepage, a great product with no distribution, a pricing page that makes no sense for the ICP. Be concrete.

Why is this a $100-worthy problem? What pattern does it represent? Is this something you've seen before, or is it new? Connect it to your developing thesis if you have one.

Why I Wanted to Work on This

What specifically about this problem matches your skills or obsessions. What you think you can see that the company might not. This is where your credibility lives — it shows you're not randomly picking companies, you're drawn to problems you're equipped to solve.

The Diagnosis

Walk through the company's growth chain:

  • Positioning: Who do they say they're for? Is it clear? Is it true?
  • Acquisition: How are customers finding them? Is it repeatable or founder-hustle?
  • Activation: What happens on first use? Do users reach value?
  • Monetization: Does the pricing match how customers get value?
  • Retention: Is there a reason to come back? Any expansion motion?

Name the earliest broken link. Explain why it's the one that matters most for their current stage. Show your evidence (screenshots, data, things you observed from the outside).

The Hypothesis

"If I [specific change], then [specific measurable outcome], because [reasoning]. I'll know within [timeframe] and I'm spending $[amount] to test it."

What I Built / Tested

The actual work. Be specific:

  • What artifacts did you create? (landing pages, ad copy, sequences, prototypes)
  • What tools did you use?
  • How long did it take?
  • Where exactly did the $[XX] go? (ad spend breakdown, tool costs, etc.)

Include screenshots, links to prototypes, recordings. The more tangible, the more credible.

Results

Hard numbers. What happened vs. what you predicted.

  • Metric 1: [expected] vs [actual]
  • Metric 2: [expected] vs [actual]
  • Total spend: $[XX]

Be honest about what worked and what didn't. If the hypothesis was wrong, say so and say why. The honesty is the whole point — it's what separates this from marketing.

What This Taught Me

The generalizable lesson. What does this tell you about GTM beyond this one company? Update your thesis. "I now believe [X] because [this experiment showed Y]."

What I'd Do Next (The Rest of the Playbook)

The experiment was the opening chapter. This is the book. If you were hired tomorrow to own this problem:

  • 30 days: [immediate actions]
  • 60 days: [build on initial results]
  • 90 days: [scale what works, cut what doesn't]

This is the section that converts attention into a conversation. Show enough that they believe you can do it; hold enough that hiring you is the only way to get the rest.
