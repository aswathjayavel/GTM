How Every Experiment Works

Same process. Every time. No shortcuts.

01

Spot

I find a company with a GTM problem that genuinely interests me — a broken funnel, a mispositioned product, an untapped channel. I write down why this problem is worth my time and money.

02

Diagnose

I walk their growth chain end-to-end: positioning, acquisition, activation, monetization, retention. I find the earliest broken link and make a falsifiable prediction about what fixing it would do.

03

Test

I spend under $100 of my own money testing the fix. Rewritten landing pages, ad variants, cold outreach sequences, onboarding prototypes — real work, real spend, real data.

04

Publish

Everything goes public. The diagnosis, the hypothesis, the spend breakdown, the results, what I got wrong. No cherry-picking. The honesty is the whole point.

Why I Do This

Most GTM advice is theory. "You should try content marketing." "Have you considered PLG?" Cool — but does it actually work for this company, at this stage, with this budget?

I got tired of opinions without receipts. So I started running the experiments myself. I pick companies whose growth problems fascinate me, spend my own money testing fixes, and publish everything — wins, failures, and the beliefs I update along the way.

The $100 constraint is deliberate. It forces creativity over budget. It proves that judgment matters more than spend. And it means every experiment I run is a bet I'm making with my own money on my own ideas.
